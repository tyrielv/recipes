# Beef Braised in "Red Wine"

*Adapted from "Mastering the Art of French Cooking"*

A 2-to-5 lb pot roast (such as rump, sirloin tip, chuck roast, top round, bottom round, or eye of round). Serves 2-3 people per pound of meat.

## Marinade

* 1 cup thinly sliced onions
* 1 cup thinly sliced carrots
* 1 cup thinly sliced celery
* 2 cloves garlic
* 1 Tb thyme
* 2 bay leaves
* 2 cloves
* 2 Tbsp dried parsley
* 1 Tbl salt
* 1/4 tsp. pepper
* 3 1/2 C red or purple grape juice
* 1 1/2 C beef bouillon (or one can)
* 1/4 C red wine vinegar
* 1/2 C olive oil

## Directions

Optionally marinate over night (otherwise just use for the sauce).

Half an hour before browning meat, remove meat from marinade and drain on a rack. Pat dry with paper towels just before browning.

Bring marinade to a boil in a sauce pan. Meanwhile, heat 4-6 Tbl vegetable oil in a skillet on medium-high. When it starts to smoke, brown the meat on all sides (10-15 minutes total).

Put meat and marinade in a baking dish or crockpot. Add beef bouillon until meat is 2/3s covered. (Optional - put in 4-6 quartered red potatoes first). Bake covered at 300º for 4 hours, or cook in crockpot on high for 6 hours, or on low for 8+ hours. Allow 30-40 minutes after the meat will have finished cooking before it needs to be served.

At least a half hour before the meat is done, start preparing [Braised Onions](braised-onions.md) (1 batch serves 3-4 people) and [Braised Carrots](braised-carrots.md) (similarly).

When meat is done, remove it from sauce and set it on a platter to rest before cutting. Set aside potatoes if they were added. Strain out the other vegetables from the sauce and throw away (you could purée them in instead, but we find it's not necessary). Boil sauce until reduced to 4-5 cups (optional - intensifies the flavor). Add 1 Tbsp cornstarch (dissolved in a small amount of water) and simmer 3 minutes. Optionally add onions and carrots and simmer 2 minutes to mix flavors, then remove onions and carrots to serving dish.
