# Black Bean Mole and Coconut Couscous

*From http://sassafrascafe.blogspot.com/2009/10/black-bean-mole-and-coconut-couscous.html*

This is **DELICIOUS**!!!!! I'm not sure if I can possibly emphasize that more (feel free to add your suggestions ;) ). Please try it sometime! Especially since it's actually pretty easy to make.

*Note: We only made half of this recipe and had enough for leftovers for one of us. Also, this recipe can be spicy if you use a hot salsa (you can also probably cut back on the chili powder in the mole if it's still too spicy).*

## Ingredients

**For the couscous:**
- 1 cup uncooked couscous
- 1/2 cup coconut, toasted
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon

**For the mole:**
- 1 TBS olive oil
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 2 (15-oz) cans black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 (16-oz) jar salsa
- 1 1/2 tsp unsweetened cocoa
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 1/4 tsp cinnamon

**For the topping:**
- 4 oz (1 cup) shredded colby jack cheese (we just used shredded cheddar)
- sour cream
- one bunch green onions, thinly sliced
- 1 medium tomato, diced

## Directions

Preheat oven to 350ºF. Place coconut on a baking sheet in a single layer. Bake for about 5 minutes, or until coconut has lightly browned. Careful not to over cook, as it will burn quickly once it starts browning!

Cook couscous according to package directions. Add toasted coconut and 1/2 tsp cinnamon. Cover and set aside, keeping warm until ready to use.

Heat olive oil in saucepan over medium heat. Saute garlic in hot oil for 1 minute, stirring constantly. Add remaining mole ingredients to saucepan and stir to combine. Bring to a simmer and reduce heat to low, stirring occasionally until some of the liquid has evaporated, about 10 minutes.

Top the coconut couscous with the black bean mixture and garnish with cheese, sour cream, green onions, and tomatoes.
