# Black Bean Soup

*Adapted from [Cooking at Home with America's Test Kitchen](https://www.amazon.com/gp/offer-listing/0936184892/)*

## Ingredients

- 1 lb dry black beans
- 4 oz ham (not cut up)
- 2 bay leaves
- 8 cups water
- 1/8 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp salt
- 3 Tbsp olive oil
- 3 cups or 2 large onions, diced
- 1/2 cup or 1 large carrot, diced
- 1 cup or 3 ribs celery, diced
- 2 Tbsp or 6 cloves minced garlic
- 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
- 1.5 Tbsp cumin
- 2 Tbsp chicken bouillon
- 2 Tbsp cornstarch
- 2 Tbsp cold water
- 2 Tbsp lime juice

## Directions

Cook beans, ham, bay leaves, water, baking soda, and salt in pressure cooker as if cooking regular dry beans. DO NOT DRAIN.

Remove ham, chop into small pieces, and set aside. Remove bay leaves and throw away.

Meanwhile, sauté onions, carrot, and celery in olive oil 15 minutes. Add garlic, pepper flakes, and cumin, and cook 3 more minutes. Add vegetables to the cooked beans, along with chicken bouillon. Simmer together 30 minutes.

Remove 1 1/2 C beans and some of the liquid, purée, then add back in. Stir in cornstarch slurry, then bring to a boil to thicken.

Remove from heat, stir in ham and lime juice. Serve with avocados and/or sour cream.
