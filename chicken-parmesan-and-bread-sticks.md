# Chicken Parmesan and Bread Sticks

This seems to be a pretty basic Chicken Parmesan recipe, and it's not too hard plus it's tasty! In all honesty, I didn't actually make the spaghetti sauce for this recipe (although I prefer my own). I just used spaghetti sauce from a jar. Saves a bit of time. :)

## Chicken Parmesan

### Ingredients

- 2 boneless-skinless chicken breasts, filleted
- 1/2 plain bread crumbs*
- 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese
- Italian Seasoning**
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp ground pepper
- 1 large egg
- 1 1/2 c [spaghetti sauce](tomato-sauce-3-spaghetti-sauce.md)
- Mozzarella and Parmesan cheese for garnishing
- olive oil, for cooking

### Directions

In a shallow bowl, combine bread crumbs, Parmesan cheese, and Italian seasoning. In a separate shallow bowl, beat egg. Coat chicken fillets on each side with egg, then coat with bread crumb mixture.

Heat oil in a sauté pan on med-high. After coating chicken in egg and bread crumbs, fry for about 5-6 minutes on each side, until chicken is browned and no longer pink inside. Add spaghetti sauce, and warm. Reduce heat, add Mozzarella cheese to tops of each chicken breast, cover, and simmer for about 3-5 minutes. Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese, and serve over noodles (spaghetti, fettuccine, etc) and, if desired, bread sticks.

## Notes

*I discovered in my pantry after making the Italian-seasoned bread crumbs that I actually had store-bought Italian-seasoned bread crumbs. Oops. Would have saved me a bit of time, but not much. :) You can use store-bought bread crumbs that are Italian-seasoned and just add Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper to it for this recipe.

**For the Italian seasoning, I just sprinkled to taste a bit of each of these: basil, oregano, thyme, ground sage, and rosemary.

## Garlic and Parmesan Bread Sticks

Preheat oven to 450ºF (with pizza stone on bottom rack, if using).

I just made half of the dough recipe for my [pizza](better-than-papa-johns.md). After letting it rest, covered, for the first 15 minutes, I rolled the dough out and cut it into the desired bread stick shapes (if you're REALLY into making it look cool, make each bread stick individually from the dough rather than just rolling it out and cutting them. I was a bit too lazy for that). Let the dough rest, covered, for another 15 or so minutes (or until you're ready to bake them).

Bake* for about 5-8 minutes, or until golden brown.

While baking, make about 2 tbsp worth of [garlic butter](garlic-butter-spread.md) (and melt it). When bread sticks are done baking, spread melted garlic butter over all the bread sticks, then sprinkle Parmesan cheese on the top.

*Helpful hint: I poke the dough several times with a fork right before it goes in the oven to help prevent huge air bubbles.
