# Chocolate Cake with Chocolate Sour Cream Frosting and Whipped Cream

This recipe is from grandma. This is the cake I like every year on my birthday!

Bake two 6" rounds from one chocolate cake mix box according to package directions (we like to use really fudgey chocolate cake mixes), and set aside to cool completely.

## Ingredients

**Whipped Cream:**
- 2 c boiling water
- 1 c heavy whipping cream
- 1 tsp vanilla (optional)
- 1 tbsp powdered sugar (optional)
- 1/2 tsp unflavored gelatin (for stabilizing whipped cream)
- 1 tbsp cold water

**Chocolate Sour Cream Frosting:**
*(1.5 times this recipe or else it's skimpy.)*
- 6oz semisweet chocolate chips (about 1 cup)
- 1/4 c butter
- 1/2 c sour cream
- 1 tsp vanilla
- pinch of salt
- 2 1/2 - 2 3/4 c powdered sugar

## Directions

**Whipped Cream:** Bring 2 cups water to a simmer in saucepan. Meanwhile, soften gelatin in 1 tablespoon cold water in coffee cup/mug. After five minutes, place cup into simmering water until gelatin is completely melted. Remove and cool until it's room temperature.

Add cream to bowl and beat on medium-high speed. As it starts to thicken, add sugar, vanilla and cooled gelatin mixture. Continue to beat until it becomes whipped cream (when the cream forms stiff peaks). See below for layering details with whipped cream.** http://www.ehow.com/how_6303441_frost-cake-whipped-cream.html

**Chocolate Sour Cream Frosting:** Melt chocolate chips and butter together; mix. Remove from heat and stir until smooth. Blend in sour cream, vanilla, and salt. Gradually stir in powdered sugar.

**Directions for layering cake:**

If cakes are rounded on top, cut top off so that cake is flat across the top. Remove cakes from pans. Poke toothpicks around the middle of the outside edge of the cake (to guide for cutting cake layer in half evenly). Take a long piece of dental floss, wrap it around the cake on top of the toothpicks, and pull all the way through the cake to cut it (see here for pictures and description: http://www.wikihow.com/Cut-a-Cake-Layer-in-Half). Remove toothpicks. You should now have four separate cakes.

**Frost the cake as soon as possible after whipping the cream. First apply a thin "crumb layer" of whipped cream as a base coat. Refrigerate whipped cream while it sits for about 15 minutes. For this layer, spread 1/4 inch of cream on the bottom layer and set the second cake layer on it. Spread the chocolate frosting for the middle layer, then place the third cake on top and frost with whipped cream as with the first layer. Lay fourth cake on top, and frost the outside of the entire cake with chocolate frosting.

**Fillings between cake layers should be in this order, top to bottom:**
- Whipped Cream
- Chocolate Sour Cream Frosting
- Whipped Cream
