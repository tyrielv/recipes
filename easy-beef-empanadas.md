# Easy Beef Empanadas

Thanks to my sister, who showed me this tasty Kraft recipe! They are fried, but next time we're going to try baking them instead for a healthier alternative and see if they turn out as well.

*From http://www.kraftrecipes.com/recipes/savory-beef-empanadas-91960.aspx and http://www.kraftrecipes.com/recipes/empanada-dough-76261.aspx*

## Ingredients

**Filling:**
- 1 lb ground beef
- 1/4 c minced onions
- 1/4 c minced green peppers
- 2 tbsp KRAFT Zesty Italian Dressing
- 2 tbsp tomato sauce (we substituted ketchup)
- 2 cloves minced garlic
- cilantro to taste
- Oil for frying

**Empanada Dough** *(Note: if you don't want to make the dough, just buy some puff pastry dough instead.)*:
- 2 c all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 c lard (I substituted with shortening)
- 1/2 c water

## Directions

**For the dough:** Combine flour, salt, and baking powder in large bowl. Cut in lard with pastry blender or 2 knives until mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add water, stirring until mixture forms a ball. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap; set aside while preparing empanada filling.

**For the filling:** Prepare empanada dough as directed; set aside. Brown meat in medium skillet on medium heat; drain. Add onions, peppers, dressing, tomato sauce, and garlic; mix well. Sauté until veggies are cooked.

Flatten dough out with fingers into ~6" circles. Spoon about 2 tbsp of the mixture into the center of each round. Moisten the edges of the dough with water. Fold dough rounds in half to enclose filling; press lightly on top to remove any air pockets. Seal edges with fork.

Heat oil in a large, deep skillet (about 1" deep of oil) to 350ºF. Add empanadas in batches of 2-4, depending on the size of pan used. Fry until golden brown on both sides. Remove from oil; drain well. Cool slightly before serving.
