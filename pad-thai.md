# Pad Thai

*Adapted from [thaitable.com](http://www.thaitable.com/Thai/recipes/Pad_Thai.htm)*

## Ingredients

- 8 oz. Thai rice noodles
- 2 Tbl. Vegetable oil
- 1 Onion, minced
- 1/2 Green bell pepper, cut in long slices
- 1 Egg
- 6 Green onions, chopped
- grated ginger
- 1 C. Bean sprouts
- 1/4 lb. cooked shrimp
- 1/4 C. Cashews, chopped

**Sauce:**
- 1/4 C. Sugar
- 1/4 C. White vinegar
- 3 Tbl. Fish sauce
- 1 tsp. Chili powder

## Directions

Soak the rice noodles in very hot (just boiled) water until flexible (about 10 minutes). In the meantime, saute the onion and vegetable oil in a large frying pan, until the onions start turning translucent. Add the bell pepper and saute another couple minutes. Drain the rice noodles and add them, as well as the sauce. Stir this all together quickly so the noodles don't stick to the pan. The noodles should absorb most of the sauce - keep sauteing until they do. Push the noodles to the side of the pan. Crack the egg into the open space and scramble it, then fold into the noodles. Add the other ingredients, mix lightly and serve.

Serve over rice and top with peanuts or cashews.

## Notes

I substituted green onions for chives, an onion for the shallot, cashews for peanuts, and vinegar for tamarind, added the green pepper, doubled the sauce and forgot the garlic. (It probably wouldn't have needed double the sauce if I had remembered the garlic. :) ) That recipe also suggested frying the nuts at the start of the recipe, but we think they would taste better added at the end.
