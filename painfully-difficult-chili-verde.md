# PAINFULLY Difficult Chili Verde

*Adapted from http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/pork-chile-verde-recipe.html*

We usually double it so we can freeze the leftovers, which requires a ~10 qt stock pot (8 qt might work but it would be a tight fit).

[Link to easier version](easy-chili-verde.md)

## Ingredients

* 2 green bell peppers
* 2 large green chili peppers, such as Anaheim, Poblano, or Hatch
* 3 jalapeño peppers
* 3 cloves minced garlic
* 1.5 lb tomatillos
* 4 lb pork shoulder, cut into 2 inch cubes
* 2 tsp salt
* 1 tsp black pepper
* Flour for dredging
* 1/4 C vegetable oil
* 3 onions, diced
* 1 Tbl dry oregano
* 2 tsp ground cumin
* 2 Tbl ground coriander
* 2 bay leaves
* 1 Tbl dry cilantro
* 4 C chicken broth
* Corn starch to thicken

## Directions

Cut all peppers in half, discard seeds and stems, and broil skin-side-up for 10-20 minutes until well charred. Place peppers in a sealed plastic bag for 20 minutes to loosen skin. Peel off charred skin, chop, and place in large stock pot.

Peel outer skin off tomatillos. Cut in half, separating the top from the bottom (rather than through the stem). Broil skin-side up for 10-20 minutes until well-charred. Peel off charred skin and add to stock pot.

Season the pork with salt and pepper. Dredge with flour. Brown in batches in skillet over medium-high (~5 minutes per side), and add to stock pot. In the same skillet, cook the onions over medium heat for 5-10 minutes until limp. If using fresh garlic, add and cook another minute.

Add onions, garlic, spices, and broth to stock pot. Bright to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer, covered, for 2-3 hours.

To thicken, either remove the cover towards the end to boil off liquid, or add corn starch (dissolved into a small amount of cold water) until desired thickness is achieved (by tablespoons, up to 1/2 C).

Serve with flour tortillas. Freezes well.
