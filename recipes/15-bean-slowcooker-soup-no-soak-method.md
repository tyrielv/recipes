# 15 Bean Slowcooker Soup - No Soak Method

*Adapted from <https://hurstbeans.com/recipes/15-bean-soup-crock-pot-or-slow-cooker-recipe>*

---

## Ingredients

- 1 pkg HamBeens® 15 BEAN SOUP®
- 8 (9-10 if you prefer a more "brothy" soup) cups water OR use chicken/beef/vegetable broth for added flavor. I used 8 cups of water and 6 tsp of chicken bouillon.
- 1 lb. smoked sausage, ground sausage (browned in pan before adding to pot), ham hocks, ham bone with some ham left on it, or diced ham.
- 1 medium size onion, diced
- 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz.)
- 1 tbsp. chili powder
- Juice from one lemon (I omitted this for a less-acidic flavor).
- Optional: Hot sauce, salt and pepper to taste

---

## Directions (No Soak Method)

1. In a colander or sieve, rinse beans thoroughly. Sort and inspect for any unwanted debris and discard.
2. Drain and pour beans in a slow cooker with 8 cups of stock/water, onions, garlic, and meat or hambone. (Not tomato or lemon juice yet!). Use 9-10 cups of liquid if you like a more "brothy" soup.
3. Stir to combine. Set slow cooker on high and cook for 6-7 hours then check to make sure beans are tender. The soup can continue to simmer for several hours and will develop more flavor over time.
4. After the beans are tender, remove the hambone. Chop or shred any ham that has fallen from the bone and add back to the pot.
5. Optional: before adding tomatoes, cook them down until most of the liquid is gone for a less acidic flavor.
6. Stir in the can of diced tomatoes, the included Ham Flavor packet, optional chili powder, and the lemon juice (we omitted the lemon juice).
7. Cook for additional 30 minutes then keep warm until ready to serve.
8. We recommend to serve with a freshly baked slice of corn bread! You can also serve over rice as well.
9. Please keep in mind that every time the lid is opened, your cooking time will be longer.

---

## Tips

- For even more flavor, substitute beef, chicken or vegetable stock instead of water.
- You can also try 1 lb. boneless chicken breasts or 1 lb. beef roast, then shred meat before Step 5 above.
- As with all dry bean recipes, it's best to wait until AFTER the beans have become tender or fully cooked before adding any acidic ingredients like tomatoes, citrus, vinegars, etc.
- You can add other vegetables like carrots & celery for even more flavor and nutrients
- Remember that this is a NO SOAK recipe, but if you have already soaked the beans that's not a problem, just use 1 cup of water/stock less.
- Variations on the recipe will work for almost all of Hurst's HamBeens items.
- If you prefer a more "brothy" soup, add an extra cup of liquid when preparing.
