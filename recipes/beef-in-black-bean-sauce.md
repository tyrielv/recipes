# Beef in Black Bean Sauce

*Adapted from "The Complete Asian Cookbook"*

## Ingredients

* 1 lb. beef (such as rump or top round)
* 1 1/2 Tbl canned black beans
* 1 Tbl soy sauce
* 1/4 C water or stock
* 1 tsp sugar
* 1 tsp cornstarch
* 2 tsp cold water
* 2 Tbl peanut or vegetable oil
* 1 tsp sesame oil
* 2 cloves garlic, crushed

## Directions

Trim fat off beef and cut into paper-thin slices (it helps to stick it in the freezer for an hour or so). If using a tough cut (like top round) then mix 1/4 tsp baking soda with 2 Tbl water, knead it into the meat, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight (supposedly this tenderizes it).

Rinse black beans and smash with a fork. Mix with soy sauce, water/stock, and sugar.

Heat oils in a wok. Fry beef over high heat until no longer red. Add the garlic and toss for a few seconds. Add the bean sauce, bring to a boil. Cover, reduce heat, and simmer for 4-5 minutes. Mix cornstarch with water and add it in. Continue cooking a minute or so to thicken. Serve immediately with rice.
