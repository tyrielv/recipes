# Beef Stew with Herb Dumplings

*From "Unofficial Harry Potter Cookbook"*

## Ingredients

1 cup flour for dumplings, 1 tbsp flour for stew

**Dumplings:**
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
- 1/4 tsp sage
- 1/4 tsp thyme
- 1/4 tsp marjoram
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 stick of butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
- 2 tbsp milk
- 1 egg

**Stew:**
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 1/2 lbs beef roast or steak
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 cups beef broth
- salt and pepper to taste
- 2 carrots, cut into 1" pieces
- 2 celery ribs, cut into 1" pieces
- 4 red-skinned potatoes, unpeeled, scrubbed, cut into 1" cubes

## Directions

To make the dumplings, whisk together flour, salt, baking powder, black pepper, and herbs. Add the pieces of butter and rub with fingertips until mixture resembles coarse meal with no powdery bits left. Whisk the milk and egg together and add to the flour mixture. Toss with a rubber spatula until mixture begins to clump together. Press plastic wrap down on top of dough and refrigerate until needed.

To make stew, sear the beef in a hot dutch oven with 1 tbsp oil. Remove and cut into 1/2" cubes. Add the remaining 2 tbsp of oil to skillet. Add onion and cook on med-low heat until softened, scraping up the fond (browned bits) from the bottom of the pot.

Add the flour to the pot and stir well. Pour in broth and stir well. Add salt, pepper, and meat with accumulated juices, and cook over med-high heat, stirring occasionally until thickened and bubbling. Reduce heat and simmer for 1 1/2 hrs.

Add carrots, celery, and potatoes to pot and simmer for 1 hr, stirring occasionally. Remove dumpling dough from refrigerator. Wet hands and form dumplings into 1" balls, dropping them into hot stew in pot as they are formed. Wet hands as needed to prevent sticking. Cover pot and simmer 30 minutes until dumplings have puffed up and feel set when pressed lightly with fingertips (though soft).
