# Cheesecake Variations

*Based on [this Food Network recipe](http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/alton-brown/sour-cream-cheesecake-recipe.html)*

I've been experimenting with Cheesecake.

## Mini Cheesecake (6-inch pan)

### Crust

Graham cracker crust in 6-inch pan (~24 squares crushed, 6 Tbl melted butter)

### Filling

- 5 oz cream cheese, softened
- 1 egg, optionally separating the white and beating to stiff peaks (we didn't like it with beaten egg whites)
- 1/3 C sour cream
- 1.5 Tbl heavy cream
- 1/4 C sugar
- 3/4 tsp vanilla

Preheat oven to 300º

Beat all filling ingredients together (except beaten egg white) until smooth. Fold in egg white. Pour into crust.

Place pan in a larger pan (e.g. 9x13 cake pan) and place in oven. Pour boiling water into the larger pan to help it cook more evenly (but not so much that it gets into the cheesecake).

Reduce temp to 250 and bake 1 hour. Turn off oven, open the door for 30 seconds, then close and leave in oven for another hour.

Remove from oven and chill in the fridge.

This made about 4 small slices. Consider doubling the filling next time - lots of room left in the pan.

## Normal Size (9-inch pan)

Use 9-inch pan (32 graham squares, 8 Tbl melted butter)

3x filling of mini: 15 oz cream cheese, 3 eggs, 1 C sour cream, 1/4 C heavy cream, 3/4 sugar, 2 1/4 tsp vanilla

Bake for 1 hour 20 minutes before turning the oven off.

I didn't beat the egg whites and this made the right amount of filling for the pan. ~8 medium or 12 small slices.

## Chocolate Variation

Add 1 Tbl cocoa to the crust, and add 6 oz melted semi-sweet chocolate to the filling (for normal size)

Note - I used an 8-inch pan for this and beat the egg whites and had about 1/3 too much filling. I also forgot the sour cream.
