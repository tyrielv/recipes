# Chicken and Cream Cheese Rolls

## Ingredients

- 2 Boneless-skinless chicken breasts, cooked and shredded
- Cream cheese (about 8oz, more or less)
- 1 Medium onion, finely chopped
- 2-3 Stalks celery, finely chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 -3 Tubes of pre-made croissant roll dough (i.e. Pillsbury or similar brand)
- 1/2 Stick butter
- Bread crumbs

## Directions

Open croissant roll dough package, carefully pull apart individual pieces, and allow dough to warm up at room temperature for a few minutes for easier handling. Set aside. In a medium bowl, combine chicken, cream cheese, onion, celery, salt, and pepper and mix well.

Once dough has warmed up and is elastic, take a small ball of chicken mixture (about 1.5-2 tbsp) and place at the large end of the dough strip. Roll chicken mixture up into the dough so that dough completely surrounds mixture (pinch any open spaces shut).

Melt butter in a dish and place bread crumbs in a separate dish. First coat the rolls in melted butter, and then in the bread crumbs so that rolls are completely covered. Place on a baking sheet. Bake at 350ºF for 20-25 minutes or until browned. Serve immediately, and enjoy!

Makes about 16 rolls.

Serves well with noodles and gravy.

## Notes

- I find it easiest to mix the recipe with my hand (gloved, of course).
- Amounts don't have to be exact; if you like more of something, add it in. Also, some people like to add peas to the recipe. I personally don't like peas, so I leave them out. It's up to you!
- If you don't want to, you don't have to shred the chicken. I just think it's easier to make the rolls with shredded chicken rather than cubed.
- I say "2-3" tubes of roll dough because 2 isn't quite enough for the entire mixture but 3 is too much dough.
- Make sure you really let your dough warm up and become elastic! It's a pain in the butt to try to make cold dough stretch over the chicken mixture because it will just tear instead of stretch.
- I also find these tasty when they're cold (i.e. leftovers).
