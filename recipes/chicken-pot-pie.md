# Chicken Pot Pie

*Pie filling adapted from <https://www.thewholesomedish.com/the-best-classic-chicken-pot-pie/>*
*Pie crust recipe passed down from family.*

---

## Filling Ingredients

- 4 tbsp butter
- 1 sliced carrots
- 1/2 c sliced celery
- 1/2 c diced onion
- 1 1/4 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp thyme
- 2 tbsp dried parsley
- 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, cubed
- 1/4 c all-purpose flour
- 1/2 c heavy cream
- 1/2 c chicken broth*
- 1/2 c frozen peas or edamame
- 2 unbaked pie crusts (1 top and 1 bottom)**

---

## Filling Directions

In a large stock pot or sauté pan, melt butter on medium-high. Add vegetables with seasonings and sauté for about 5 minutes, until vegetables begin to soften. Add chicken and sauté until cooked through. Add flour. Stir until no dry flour remains.

Slowly stir in cream, then broth. Cook until bubbling and thick, 3-4 minutes. *Add additional broth if it becomes too thickened.

Remove pan from heat and stir in peas/edamame. Allow filling to cool for 15-30 minutes. Preheat oven to 425⁰F.

Once cooled, pour filling an unbaked pie crust, top with additional unbaked pie crust and press crust edges together to seal. Cut vents in top crust. Brush milk or an egg wash with a pastry brush over the top crust. Bake pie for 15 minutes, then turn the oven temperature down to 350⁰F for another 30 minutes until crust is a nice golden brown on top.

---

## No-Fail Pie Crust

*(This can be made while filling is cooling and oven is pre-heating.)*

### Ingredients

- 2 1/2 c flour
- 1 tsp garlic salt (if you're using this pie crust for dessert, use plain salt)
- 1 c shortening
- 1/4 c water (as cold as you can get it)
- 1 egg
- 1 tbsp vinegar

### Directions

Sift together flour and salt, then cut in shortening. Set aside. Mix together all liquids, and stir into flour mixture.

Separate dough into two balls and roll out two thin pastry crusts. Bake as directed above.

---

## Notes

1. Be careful not to over-mix the dough so as to maintain a nice, flaky texture after baking.
2. Pouring hot filling into the unbaked pie crust will make the crust soggy. You could make the filling up to 3 days ahead of time and store it in a sealed container in the refrigerator.
3. I like to turn my pie 180⁰ around in my oven when I turn the temperature down because my oven doesn't heat evenly. This prevents one side from getting over-done and the other side not getting done enough.
