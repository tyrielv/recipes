# Chili Colorado

*Adapted from "Mexican Family Cooking" by Aida Gabilondo*

## Ingredients

For each lb beef or pork:

- 2 ancho (or similar) dried chilies
- 3 new mexico (or similar) dried chilies
- 2 cloves garlic
- 1/4 C lard
- 2 Tbsp flour
- 3/4 tsp salt + more to taste
- 1/2 tsp oregano
- 1/2 tsp cumin

## Directions

Cook the beef or pork in a crockpot on low all day (8-10 hours) with 1/2 C water and sprinkled with salt. Reserve the broth.

Remove tops and seeds from chilies. Boil in salted water (just enough to cover) for 15 minutes, then drain. Purée with garlic and broth from the meat. Add water as necessary to make 2 C per lb of meat.

Melt lard in a sauce pan large enough to hold the chili purée. Stir in flour and cook for 5-10 minutes until golden brown. Stir in chili purée and seasonings, and simmer for 10 more minutes. Shred or cube meat and stir in.
