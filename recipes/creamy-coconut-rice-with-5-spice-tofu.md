# Creamy Coconut Rice with 5 Spice Tofu

*Adapted from <https://rainbowplantlife.com/creamy-coconut-rice-with-five-spice-tofu/#recipe>*

**Prep time:** 25 minutes
**Cook time:** 35 minutes
**Total:** 1 hr

---

## Ingredients

**Five Spice Tofu**

- 1 lb super-firm tofu (or extra-firm tofu) (Note 1)
- 1 heaping teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 heaping teaspoon Chinese five spice powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 2 tablespoons neutral-flavored oil

**Rice and Vegetables**

- 1 1/2 tablespoons neutral-flavored oil
- 1 bunch scallions (about 6), sliced thinly (save dark greens for garnish)
- 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 ½ inch piece ginger, minced or grated
- 1 tsp chili flakes
- 4 medium carrots scrubbed and chopped (or 2 heaping cups small cauliflower florets)
- 1-2 teaspoons dry cilantro
- 1 teaspoon Chinese five spice powder
- ½ teaspoon ground pepper
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- ¼ teaspoon ground turmeric
- ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 cup medium-grain white rice (e.g., Calrose or arborio rice)
- 1 ½ cups vegetable or chicken broth
- 1 (13.5-ounce) can full-fat coconut milk
- 1 tablespoon tamari or soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon honey
- 1 small or medium red (or orange or yellow) bell pepper, thinly sliced (or minced for the kids' sakes)
- 2/3 cup roasted, salted peanuts or cashews, roughly chopped
- 1 medium lime, zested and juiced (about 1 tablespoon of lime juice)

---

## Instructions

1. Prep the tofu. Slice the tofu into 4 vertical slabs. For super firm tofu, gently squeeze each slab to remove excess water. For extra firm tofu, cover the slabs with a thin dish towel or a few paper towels; weigh down with a heavy cookbook; press for 10 minutes, or while you prep the aromatics and vegetables. Boneless, skinless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces, can easily be used in place of tofu.
2. In a sieve, rinse and drain rice until the water turns clear. It'll take a few rounds of rinsing.
3. Preheat the oven to 375ºF.
4. Season the tofu or chicken. For tofu, tear into chunks, not too big or small. Transfer to a large bowl. In a small bowl, mix together the salt, five spice powder, and onion powder. Sprinkle this over the tofu or chicken, using your hands to gently coat the protein.
5. Cook the tofu. (See Note 2.) Heat the 2 TBSP oil in a 12-inch nonstick frying pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the tofu and spread out in a single packed layer. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes undisturbed. Flip and continue cooking for a total of 12 to 14 minutes, stirring only every 2 minutes, until most of the tofu is browned. For chicken, pan fry seasoned chicken until golden.
6. While the tofu/chicken cooks, heat a Dutch oven or other oven-safe pot (at least 3-quart/3L) with the oil over medium-high heat. Add the scallion whites and light greens, garlic, ginger, chili flakes, and carrots. Cook, stirring frequently, for 2 to 3 minutes. Add the five spice powder, white pepper, coriander, turmeric, kosher salt, and stir almost constantly for 30 seconds.
7. Add the rice and stir to evenly coat each grain. Pour in the vegetable broth, coconut milk, tamari, honey, bell pepper, and peanuts. Increase the heat, cover the pan, and bring to a boil.
8. Take off the heat, add in the cooked tofu/chicken, stir to combine, and cover the pan with its lid (or tightly cover with foil if you don't have a lid).
9. Transfer the pan to the oven and bake, covered, for 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender.
10. Take out of the oven and stir in the cilantro, reserved scallion greens, lime zest, and 1 tablespoon lime juice. Season to taste with a few pinches of salt and pepper, adding more lime juice as needed.

---

## Notes

- **Note 1:** Super firm tofu is really lovely here, giving the tofu a meaty, chewy texture, a great contrast to the creamy rice.
- **Note 2:** If you don't have a large nonstick frying pan, fry the tofu in two batches for the best texture.
