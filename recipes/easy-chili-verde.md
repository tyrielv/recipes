# Easy Chili Verde

An easier (but unfortunately less tasty) version of [PAINFULLY Difficult Chili Verde](painfully-difficult-chili-verde.md). Ingredients are pretty much the same, but prepared in a less labor-intensive fashion. Main differences:

* Pork is pre-cooked and shredded instead of cubed
* Peppers and tomatillos are not pre-roasted

If you have time to do just one thing from the "difficult" recipe, roast the tomatillos - it has the biggest effect on the flavor, making it much less acidic.

## Ingredients

* 4 lb pork shoulder
* 2 tsp salt
* 1 tsp black pepper
* 1/2 C water
* (optional) 1 tsp liquid smoke
* 1 Tbl vegetable oil
* 3 onions, diced
* 2 green bell peppers, seeded and cut in 1-inch cubes
* 2 large green chili peppers, such as Anaheim, Poblano, or Hatch, seeded and cut in 1-inch cubes
* 3 jalapeño peppers, seeded and diced
* 3 cloves minced garlic
* 1.5 lb tomatillos, chopped
* 1 Tbl dry oregano
* 2 tsp ground cumin
* 2 Tbl ground coriander
* 2 bay leaves
* 1 Tbl dry cilantro
* 4 C chicken broth
* Corn starch to thicken, up to 1/2 C

## Directions

Cook the pork shoulder in a crockpot overnight with salt, pepper, water, and liquid smoke. Shred it, and return to crockpot (along with all drippings).

Cook the onions in oil over medium heat for 5-10 minutes until limp. Add the peppers and continue cooking 5 minutes. If using fresh garlic, add and cook for another minute.

Add all ingredients (except corn starch) to crock pot and stir well. Cook on low all day. Alternatively, bring to a boil in a regular stock pot and simmer, covered, for at least 2 hours.

Thicken with corn starch to desired thickness (by tablespoons, up to 1/2 C). Serve with tortillas. Freezes well.
