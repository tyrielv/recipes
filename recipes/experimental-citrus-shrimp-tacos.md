# Experimental Citrus Shrimp Tacos

There was a sale on shrimp today at Safeway, so I decided to experiment and it turned out pretty well. (Well enough that they were all eaten before we thought to take a picture :) )

## Ingredients

- 1/2 lb shrimp, shelled (We used raw, but precooked would probably be fine)
- 1/2 can crushed pineapple
- 1 Tbl olive oil
- 1 squirt each of lime juice, lemon juice, and orange juice
- 1 tsp minced garlic
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 1/2 onion, cut into longish strips
- 1/2 green pepper, cut into longish strips
- A few dashes of seasoning salt (or salt and pepper)
- A dash of red wine vinegar
- Tortillas (about 4)
- Refried beans (optional)
- Sour cream (optional)
- Chopped lettuce (optional)
- Salsa (optional)
- Shredded cheese (optional)

## Directions

Mix the pineapple, garlic, salt, olive oil, and citrus juices in a bowl to make a marinade. Marinate the shrimp for at least 20 minutes - longer is better. Optional: If the shrimp have finished marinating before you start the rest of the recipe, you can remove the shrimp and boil the marinade while you cook the onion and green pepper for a fantastic sauce.

Sauté the onion and green pepper in a bit of vegetable oil for about 10 minutes (until flexible but not slimy). Season with a few dashes of seasoning salt and red wine vinegar while you sauté. Remove and set aside.

Remove the shrimp from the marinade, and fry just until thoroughly cooked.

Warm the tortillas (and refried beans) in the microwave. Set out the shrimp, onions and green peppers, and any other ingredients where everyone can reach, and make into tacos.

Serves 2 with no leftovers (at least of the main ingredients).
