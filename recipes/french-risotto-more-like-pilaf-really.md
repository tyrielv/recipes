# French "Risotto" (More like a pilaf, really)

*Adapted from "Mastering the Art of French Cooking"*

## Ingredients

- 1/4 minced onion
- 4 Tbl butter
- 1 1/2 C white rice (uncooked)
- 3 C boiling liquid, depending on what it will be served with (e.g. chicken broth, beef broth, fish stock, or just water)
- 1 tsp parsley
- 1/3 bay leaf
- 1/8 tsp thyme
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Directions

Preheat oven to 375ºF. In a 2+-quart oven-safe pan, sauté onions in butter for 5 minutes. Stir in rice and cook over moderate heat, stirring constantly, until rice turns translucent then milky (but not until brown).

Pour in the boiling liquid and add herbs. Bring to a simmer, stir, then cover and set in lower-third of preheated oven*. Once the liquid comes back to a simmer, reduce heat to 350. Cook 18 minutes. Remove from oven, and tilt to see if the liquid is absorbed. If not, return to oven for 2-3 more minutes.

Once liquid is absorbed, for al dente rice leave uncovered. For softer, leave covered for 10-20 more minutes. Fluff rice and season to taste before serving.

## Notes

*It's possible to do this on the stove on low, but the rice on the bottom of the pan may stick and burn.
