# Green Chili Pork Burritos, Enchilada Style

Fits in a 9x13 pan.

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs pork roast or pork tenderloin
- 1/4 c water
- 1/2 tbsp salt
- pepper
- 2 c (~14oz canned) green chilies, chopped
- 1/2 of a 28oz can green chili enchilada sauce (save the rest)
- 1/2 onion, chopped
- 1/2 tsp cumin or Mexican spices
- Handful of fresh cilantro
- 1/2 c drippings from roast
- Corn tortillas
- Remaining half of enchilada sauce
- Cheddar cheese, shredded

## Directions

Cook the pork roast in a Crock-Pot with water, salt, and pepper overnight on low. Remove the roast, reserving the liquid. Cube or shred meat and place back in the liquid in the Crock-Pot. Add the remaining ingredients (reserving half the enchilada sauce) and let simmer on high, uncovered, for 2-3 hours to reduce the liquid and allow the mixture to thicken. Stir occasionally and watch the consistency; since the meat will be spooned into tortillas, it should have the thickness of a stew. If the mixture starts getting too thick, add additional drippings and/or cover it. If the mixture is too thin when ready to serve, thicken with a little cornstarch.

Place a large spoonful into a warmed tortilla (if using corn tortillas, which go extremely well with this recipe, make sure you heat them nicely up just a few at a time so they don't crack as easily), and wrap. Place burritos in a 9x13 baking pan in a row (note, we actually had enough to make one 9x13 and one 8x8 pan). Once all burritos are in place, cover with the remaining enchilada sauce and sprinkle with cheese. Bake in preheated oven at 400ºF for about 15-20 minutes, or until sauce bubbles. Serve immediately with rice, black beans, sour cream, guacamole, and whatever else you like with enchiladas!
