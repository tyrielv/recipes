# Hummus

*Adapted from http://www.inspiredtaste.net/15938/easy-and-smooth-hummus-recipe/*

## Ingredients

* 1 cup dry chickpeas, cooked*
* 1/4 cup lemon juice
* 1/4 cup tahini
* 1/4 tsp minced garlic
* 2 T olive oil
* 1/2-1 tsp salt, depending upon taste
* 1/2 tsp cumin
* 2-3 T water

## Directions

In the bowl of a food processor**, combine tahini and lemon juice. Process for 1 minute. Scrape sides and bottom of bowl then turn on and process for 30 seconds. This extra time helps "whip" or "cream" the tahini, making smooth and creamy hummus possible.

Add the olive oil, minced garlic, cumin and the salt to the whipped tahini and lemon juice mixture. Process for 30 seconds, scrape sides and bottom of bowl then process another 30 seconds.

Add half of the chickpeas to the food processor then process for 1 minute. Scrape sides and bottom of bowl, add remaining chickpeas and process for 1 to 2 minutes or until thick and quite smooth.

Most likely the hummus will be too thick or still have tiny bits of chickpea. To fix this, with the food processor turned on, slowly add 2 to 3 tablespoons of water until the consistency is perfect.

## Notes

*The original recipe called for a 15oz can of chickpeas, which is about 1.7ish cups. 1 cup of dry chickpeas is over two cups cooked. As a result, I suspect the tahini/hummus flavor probably wasn't as strong as it would be in the original recipe. Since I usually think that flavor is too strong, extra chickpeas was perfect for me.

**I don't have a food processor, and the amount of food in this recipe was too little for my regular blender. Instead, I used my immersion blender. The consistency probably wasn't as smooth as it might have gotten in a food processor, but it was good enough for me.
