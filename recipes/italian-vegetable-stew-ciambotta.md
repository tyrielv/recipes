# Italian Vegetable Stew (Ciambotta)

*Adapted from [America's Test Kitchen](https://www.americastestkitchen.com/recipes/6951-italian-vegetable-stew-ciambotta)*

## Ingredients

### Pestata

- 1/3 C chopped fresh basil, or 2 Tbsp dry
- 1/3 C chopped fresh oregano, or 2 Tbsp dry
- 2 Tbsp olive oil
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes

### Stew

- 1 eggplant (~3/4 lb), peeled and chopped
- 1 1/2 tsp salt
- 2 Tbsp olive oil
- 1 lb potatoes, peeled and chopped
- 1 large onion, peeled and chopped
- Additional 1 Tbsp olive oil
- 2 Tbsp tomato paste
- 28 oz canned diced tomatoes
- 2 C water
- Additional 1 Tbsp olive oil
- 2 zucchini, chopped
- 2 red or yellow bell peppers, chopped
- Additional 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 batch Pestata
- 1/4 C water
- 1 C chopped fresh basil (optional)

## Directions

Blend pestata ingredients and set aside.

Toss eggplant with salt. Set on two layers of paper towels on a large plate or bowl. Microwave 8-12 minutes until dry to the touch and wrinkly.

Heat 2 Tbsp olive oil in dutch oven until shimmering. Sauté eggplant, onion, and potatoes 2-3 minutes. Push to side, and add 1 Tbsp olive oil and 2 Tbsp tomato paste in the open space. Stir constantly for 1 minute. Add diced tomatoes and water, scraping the bottom to get any fond loose. Bring to a boil, reduce heat and cover. Let simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until eggplant has disintegrated.

Meanwhile, heat 1 Tbsp oil in a 12-inch skillet on high heat until oil smokes. Add zucchini, bell pepper, and salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 10-12 minutes. Vegetables should be well browned. Push to side. Add pestata to open space and let it soften a minute. Stir together, then scrape out into a bowl. Add 1/4 C water and scrape to deglaze pan, then pour into the bowl.

When vegetables in dutch oven are ready, stir in the other bowl of vegetables. Remove from heat and let sit 20 minutes to mingle flavors. Stir in fresh basil just before serving, if using.
