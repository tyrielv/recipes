# Massaman Curry

*Adapted from http://www.thaitable.com/thai/recipe/beef-massaman-curry and http://www.thaitable.com/thai/recipe/massaman-curry-paste.*

## Ingredients

- 3 Tbl massaman curry paste (see substitute below)
- 1 can coconut milk*
- 2 chicken breasts, cut into small pieces
  (or substitute 1 can chickpeas or 1 cup dry chick peas, cooked, per chicken breast)
- 2-3 potatoes, diced
- 1-2 onions, chopped
- bay leaf
- 3 C water
- 1 Tbl salt
- 1 Tbl sugar
- 1 Tbl tamarind paste
- 1 Tbl lime juice
- 3/4 C cashews

**Massaman Curry Paste substitute:**
- 1 Tbl paprika
- 1 1/2 tsp minced garlic
- 3/4 tsp ground coriander
- 3/4 tsp ground nutmeg
- 3/4 tsp lemongrass paste (optional?)
- 3/4 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp ground cardamom
- 1/4 tsp ground cumin
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp shrimp paste (optional?)
- 1/8 tsp ground cloves

## Directions

Boil potatoes for 5 minutes; drain. Meanwhile, mix curry paste into coconut milk and bring to a simmer over medium heat. Add the chicken and cook, stirring frequently, until chicken is cooked. Add onions, potatoes, and chickpeas (if used) and simmer for a couple minutes. Add water and bay leaf, bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 60 minutes, or until onions are soft and potatoes are flavorful throughout. Stir in tamarind, sugar, salt, lime juice, and cashews. Serve over rice.

*Alternatively, we have used 2 cans of coconut milk and no water, threw everything into a slow cooker, and cooked it on low for 6-8 hours. I preferred it this way over the other way.
