# No-Bake Chewy Granola Bars

The original recipe (found at http://happymoneysaver.com/no-bake-homemade-chewy-granola-bars/) called for an additional half cup of honey and a half cup of brown sugar as well. I thought it was way too sugary for my tastes (although the kids loved it), and I wanted something with a bit more substance. I replaced the 1/2 cup brown sugar and 1/2 cup of the honey with 1 cup of peanut butter. It's a bit more like a protein bar like this, and it does slow the kids down from snarfing all the granola bars in one sitting (more filling, I suppose). I also thought the original recipe didn't have NEARLY enough oats, so I upped it from 2 2/3 cups to 4 cups. The bars do tend to fall apart a bit more as a result, but not too badly.

## Ingredients

* 1 cup honey
* 1/3 cup shortening
* 1/4 tsp salt
* 2 cups peanut butter
* 2 tsp. vanilla extract
* 3 cups crisped rice cereal
* 3 cups old fashioned oats
* 1/2 – 1 cups chocolate chips

## Directions

In a large bowl, combine your oats and crispy rice cereal. Stir with a large spoon until well combined. Set aside.

Measure out honey, shortening, peanut butter, and salt and place in a sauce pan over medium heat. Stirring the whole time, heat until mixture combines smoothly. Take pan off the heat and add in the vanilla extract. Stir until smooth. Pour over the bowl of oat/crispy rice cereal mixture. Stir until well combined. Let it sit and cool for about 5-8 minutes.

Meanwhile, get your cookie sheet lined with parchment paper (apparently greasing the sheet doesn't work very well, but I've never bothered to try because I always have parchment paper on hand). After the mixture has cooled enough that a) you won't get burned by touching it and b) the chocolate chips won't melt upon contact, pour the granola mixture out onto the cookie sheet. Spread evenly.

Next, tear off another large piece of parchment paper and place over the granola mixture. Take another cookie sheet of the same size and place it on top of the parchment. Press down. This will help to compact the granola so that the bars will stay together and not fall apart. Little helpers love to do this part. I actually set both pans on the floor for this part and let them stomp on it.

Remove top cookie sheet and parchment, sprinkle chocolate chips evenly over the top and repeat the process above, taking the parchment paper and extra cookie sheet and pressing the two together so that the chocolate chips get pressed into the bars. Remove parchment paper and cookie sheet.

If you have fridge space, place cookie sheet in the fridge to finish cooling. If not, it can finish cooling on the counter just as well. Once the bars have cooled completely, slide the whole thing out of the pan. Cut the bars into the size of your choosing.

The bars will probably be good for about a week or so in the fridge. However, to help them last longer, I plastic wrap each bar individually, then put them all in a freezer bag in the freezer. That way they will last longer and you can just grab a bar on the go and it's already wrapped up for you to take somewhere (and they don't take long to thaw).
