# Okonomiyaki

The first time I was told we were having "cabbage pancakes" for dinner, I about gagged. Who wants to eat a pancake with cabbage? But! I was brave and tried it. It's really good! And considering that it didn't make me sick and I felt very satisfied after eating, that's a major bonus! You should try it!

## Ingredients

- 2 c all-purpose flour
- 2 tsp baking powder (optional)
- 1 1/3 c chicken broth (we actually did 1 1/2 tsp chicken bouillon and 2/3 c water)
- 4 eggs
- 6 c shredded/chopped cabbage
- 2 c raw shrimp (or other meat of your choice - we usually do half the batch with shrimp and the other half of the batch with chicken)*, chopped
- About 12 slices of bacon, cut in half

## Directions

Combine flour, baking powder, broth, and eggs and mix until blended like a pancake batter. Add cabbage and shrimp/meat, and stir well. Heat large skillet to 400ºF with a thin layer of oil. Lay 1-2 half strips of bacon next to each other and spread cabbage batter in pancake-sized circles about 3/4 of an inch thick on top. Do this for each griddle cake. Cook for 4 minutes on the first side, flip and cook for another 4 minutes on the other side. Add more oil as needed.

Serve with Japanese mayo and okonomi sauce.
