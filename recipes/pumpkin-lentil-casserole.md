# Pumpkin-Lentil Casserole

This is one of a handful of recipes that we still make from when I did a Vegan diet for a month for a college course project. Between the pumpkin or squash and the lentils it provides complete protein, and it's also super-cheap when squash is in season. :)

## Ingredients

- 2 Tbl. butter or vegetable oil
- 1 med. onion, chopped
- 1 cup lentils (dry)
- Water as needed
- 3 cups cubed (bite-size) pumpkin or other winter squash (this is about half of a large acorn squash)
- 1 Tbl. lemon juice
- 1 tsp parsley flakes
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp ground ginger
- 1/4 tsp ground pepper
- 1/4 tsp ground cumin

## Directions

1. In a large sauce pan, sauté the onion in the butter or vegetable oil until translucent, about 5 minutes.
2. Stir in the lentils. Add enough water to cover the lentils. Cover the pan and bring to a boil, then turn down the heat and simmer until the lentils are barely tender (~20 minutes).
3. While the lentils are cooking, you can cube the pumpkin/squash. I usually microwave it for a minute to soften it up.
4. Add the pumpkin/squash, lemon juice, and spices. Also add a little bit of water if it's too dry - you don't want it soupy at all, but you don't want it to burn either. Cover the pan again and cook for another 20 minutes, or until the pumpkin/squash is tender.

Serves 4.
