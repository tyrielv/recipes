# Salmon Croquettes

*Adapted from "100% Natural Lard: The Lost Art of Cooking with Your Grandmother's Secret Ingredient"*

## Ingredients

* 2 Tbl butter
* 4 Tbl flour
* 3/4 tsp salt
* pinch of black pepper
* 1 C milk
* ~1 lb. cooked, deboned salmon (canned is ok)
* 2 Tbl fresh parsley or 1 Tbl dry
* 1/2 tsp lemon juice
* Lard, for frying
* 1-2 eggs, slightly beaten (start with 1)
* 2 Tbl water per egg
* ~3/4 C bread crumbs

## Directions

White sauce: melt butter over low heat, whisk in flour, salt, and pepper, cook 3 minutes, slowly whisk in milk and cook until thickened.

Combine salmon, parsley, lemon juice, and white sauce in a mixing bowl. Add more salt and pepper to taste. Cover and refrigerate until firm.

Heat lard in a skillet over medium high (Note: we had to turn it down to medium low to prevent burning), 1/4" deep.

Form the salmon mixture into patties (~8 of them - think small hamburger patty). Dredge in bread crumbs, dip in egg, then dip in bread crumbs again. Fry for 2-3 minutes per side until golden brown, then drain on paper towels.

Goes well with tartar sauce.
