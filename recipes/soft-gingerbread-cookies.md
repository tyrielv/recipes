# Soft Gingerbread Cookies

*Adapted from [Brown Eyed Baker](http://www.browneyedbaker.com/gingerbread-men-cookies/)*

Yield: six to seven 3" gingerbread men cookies, 1/4" thick

## Ingredients

- 3/4 c flour
- 3 tbsp brown sugar
- 3/4 tsp cinnamon
- 3/4 tsp ginger
- 1/8 tsp ground cloves
- 1/8 tsp salt
- 1/8 tsp baking soda
- 3 tbsp butter, softened
- 3 tbsp molasses
- 1 1/2 tsp milk

## Directions

Stir together flour, sugar, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, salt and baking soda until combined. Add butter pieces and blend (I used a pastry blender) until mixture is sandy and resembles fine meal. Gradually add molasses and milk; mix until dough is evenly moistened, about 20 seconds. Add more flour as necessary until dough is still soft but doesn't stick terribly to your fingers.

Scrape dough onto work surface; divide in half. Working with one portion of dough at a time, roll ¼-inch thick between two large sheets of parchment paper. Leaving dough sandwiched between parchment layers, stack on cookie sheet and freeze until firm, 15 to 20 minutes. (Alternatively, refrigerate dough 2 hours or overnight.)

Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.

Remove one dough sheet from freezer; place on work surface. Peel off top parchment sheet and lay it back in place. Flip dough over; peel off and discard second parchment layer. Cut dough into gingerbread people or round cookies, transferring shapes to parchment-lined cookie sheets with a wide metal spatula, spacing them ¾-inch apart. Repeat with remaining dough until cookie sheet is full. Bake cookies until set in centers and dough barely retains imprint when touched very gently with fingertip, 8 to 11 minutes, rotating cookie sheet from front to back halfway through baking time. Do not overbake. Cool cookies on sheets 2 minutes, then remove with wide metal spatula to wire rack; cool to room temperature.
