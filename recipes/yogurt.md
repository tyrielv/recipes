# Yogurt

## Ingredients

* 1 gallon milk (You can make less at a time, but it's much easier to keep the temperature in the right range with a whole gallon)
* ~1/2 C plain yogurt
* ~4 C pureed fruit (~ 2 lbs), such as strawberries or peaches
* (optional) honey or sugar to taste

## Directions

Heat milk to 180ºF. Using the microwave is easiest as it won't burn on the bottom. Our microwave takes 35-40 minutes. This step sterilizes the milk, so that the yogurt bacteria added in the next step are the only ones growing during incubation.

Allow milk to cool to 120ºF. Stir in plain yogurt. Incubate in oven for 8-12 hours, until it's clearly yogurt curds and whey (NOT milky) when stirred. For best results, keep the milk temperature between 110ºF and 120ºF, by warming the oven to its lowest setting (170ºF on ours) at the start and whenever it's cooled. If the milk gets below 100º, reheating it in the microwave is more effective - just don't let it get above 120ºF.

Save 1/2 C of the yogurt in the freezer to use for next time.

For thicker yogurt, strain off the whey. The best material we've found is an old t-shirt, cut across the middle (armpit to armpit) and sewn to make a bag, then stretched over a large stock pot and held in place with a big rubber band.

Straining can take anywhere from 30 minutes to 4 hours depending on how fully the yogurt has set up, and will typically produce about 7-10 cups of whey. Don't throw away the whey until finished, in case the yogurt turns out too thick.

Blend in the pureed fruit until smooth. For even thicker yogurt, you can reduce the puree in a saucepan over low heat for a few hours (while straining). For thinner yogurt, blend back in some of the strained whey.

If the yogurt is too tart (e.g. strawberries), add sugar or honey to taste (1/4 C is probably more than enough).

Leftover whey can be used in baked goods in place of water.
