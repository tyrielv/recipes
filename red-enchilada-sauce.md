# Red Enchilada Sauce

*Adapted from "Daily Tortilla"*

## Ingredients

* 1 ~3oz bag (10-15) large dried chilies, such as ancho or New Mexico (Note for next time: New Mexico are a little bit more spicy than we prefer)
* water: 6 cups, 3 cups, 2 cups
* 3 cloves minced garlic
* 8 oz. can tomato sauce
* 2 tsp salt
* 4 Tbl lard, butter or shortening
* 1/3 C flour

## Directions

Heat oven to 400º (Note for next time: Try broiling to avoid preheating). Remove stems and seeds from dried chilies. Roast for ~3 minutes, just until they start to smoke.

Add to 6 cups boiling water. Reduce heat to simmer and simmer for 10 minutes.

Drain liquid and transfer chilies to blender. Add 3 cups fresh water, garlic, tomato sauce, and salt. Purée.

(At this point pressing through a fine strainer to remove any lingering seeds or chunks is an option, but we didn't find this necessary)

Melt lard (or other fat) in a sauce pan. Whisk in flour and cook for ~2 minutes to make a rue. Slowly stir in sauce. Simmer on medium for 5 minutes or until starting to thicken.

Makes 2 quarts.
