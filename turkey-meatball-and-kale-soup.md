# Turkey Meatball and Kale Soup

*Adapted from Lake Hills Ward, 2022*

---

## Meatball Ingredients

- 1 lb ground turkey
- 1 egg, beaten
- 2-4 tbsp flour
- 2 cloves minced garlic (or 1/4 tsp garlic powder)
- 1/2 tsp each OR 2 tbsp fresh and chopped:
  - dried parsley
  - basil
  - dill
  - cilantro
- 3/4 tsp salt
- several grinds of pepper (or a dash of pre-ground pepper)
- 2 tbsp butter or oil (for browning)

## Meatball Directions

In a large bowl, combine ground turkey, eggs, flour, garlic, herbs, and salt/pepper and mix until well incorporated. Add just enough flour to the meat so it is not too sticky to roll into balls.

Roll into small 1" balls. Heat 2 tbsp of butter or oil in a large skillet on medium-high heat. Cook meatballs 3-4 minutes until all sides are brown. You may need to do this in batches. set aside.

---

## Soup Ingredients

- 2 tbsp butter or oil
- 1 onion, chopped
- 4 large carrots, sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 bay leaves
- 6 cups chicken or vegetable broth**
- pre-browned meatballs
- 1 bunch of kale - washed, destemmed, and chopped

## Soup Directions

Heat 2 tbsp butter or oil in a large stockpot. Add onion, carrots, and garlic and cook until onions are translucent, about 5 minutes. Add bay leaves, broth, and meatballs. Bring to a boil, then turn down heat and simmer for 5 minutes. Add kale and cook until meatballs are cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.

---

## Notes

**We used our own chicken/vegetable stock by putting the following into a stockpot:

- leftover chicken bones/skin/fat (usually leftovers from a whole, deboned rotisserie chicken)
- 1 onion chopped in half
- 1 carrot cut in half
- 1 stick of celery cut in half
- enough water to cover the chicken and vegetables

Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 2 hours, covered.

Strain the chicken and vegetables out of the stock when done, skim off the fat (we use a gravy/fat separator measuring cup), and add 1/4 tsp of salt per cup of chicken stock. We don't usually add salt until we actually use it. Extra can be portioned into freezer bags and frozen for later use.

You could also substitute with chicken bouillion if you don't have chicken stock.
