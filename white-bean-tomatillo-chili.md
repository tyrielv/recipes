# White Bean-Tomatillo Chili

## Ingredients

- 1 lb dry white beans (if you're not doing this in a pressure cooker, I would recommend 6 cups worth of canned beans, drained and rinsed)
- 1 tbsp canola oil
- 2 c chopped yellow onion
- 1 8oz can diced green chilies
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 1/2 tbsp dried oregano
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 5 c chicken broth if using pre-cooked beans, or 7 c if using dry uncooked beans
- 1 24oz can tomatillos or 28oz can tomatoes, drained and chopped
- 1 c chopped fresh cilantro (or a couple of tbsp dried and chopped)
- 1 c chopped scallion
- 1 tbsp lime juice
- Topping: Sour cream, salsa, and sprig of parsley or cilantro

## Directions

Place beans in a large bowl and add enough water to cover them by several inches. Allow the beans to set for 8-12 hours. Drain the beans.

**Pressure cooker instructions:** Heat oil in the pressure cooker pot. Add the onion and sauté over medium heat for 5 minutes. Add the chilies, garlic, oregano, cumin, and chili powder and sauté the mixture for 5 minutes longer. Add the beans, broth, tomatillos, and cilantro. Lock pressure cooker lid into place. Cook on High Pressure for 25-30 minutes. Allow pressure cooker to do a natural pressure release. Once pressure is down, take lid off and simmer the chili until desired consistency (stirring occasionally). Add scallions and lime juice at the end and heat through. Pour a spoonful of salsa and sour cream on top of each serving of chili and garnish with a sprig of fresh cilantro or parsley, if desired.

**Stove-top instructions:** Heat oil in a large stockpot. Add the onion and sauté over medium heat for 5 minutes. Add the chilies, garlic, oregano, cumin, and chili powder and sauté the mixture for 5 minutes longer. Add the beans, broth, tomatillos, and chopped cilantro. Bring mixture to a boil, reduce heat, and let the mixture simmer for 45 min-1 hr if using canned beans, or about 3 hrs if using dried beans, stirring occasionally. Add the scallion and lime juice at the end and stir to heat through. Pour a spoonful of salsa and sour cream on top of each serving of chili and garnish with a sprig of fresh cilantro or parsley, if desired.
